The problem

Great tools ship with READMEs written by people who already get it — so a curious developer hits a wall of jargon and quietly closes the tab.

Not because the tool isn’t good — because the introduction isn’t. Engineers ship things assuming people already know why they’d care. They usually don’t. Repo Explainer’s whole job is to cure that.

How it’s made great

Unique by default. Reliably great by construction.

Every explainer is its own thing — its own metaphor, palette and voice — but none of them ship until they clear the same hard bar.

Genuinely responsive

Designed mobile-first and verified on real pixels at 390 px and 1440 px — not assumed.

Grounded in real source

Every claim is traceable to the repo’s actual code via a real vector knowledge base. No invented features.

Real diagrams, not decoration

Each page carries a true architecture diagram and a data-flow diagram, drawn from the repo’s real structure.

Accessible & semantic

Landmarks, keyboard focus, alt text and AA contrast are built into the shared system every page composes onto.

95min on both devices

Before it ships, a vision model scores each page on two rubrics — “do they get it?” (substance) and “did someone who gives a shit make this?” (craft) — on mobile and desktop. Nothing ships until every line clears the bar. The real test: a developer looks at it and smiles.
